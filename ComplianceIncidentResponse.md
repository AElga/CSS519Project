# ElgHealth Breach and Incident Response

This document records the minimum incident handling process for privacy, security, and breach-notification events affecting ElgHealth.

## Trigger Conditions

Treat the event as a security incident when any of the following occur:

- Unauthorized access to patient, appointment, or clinical record data is suspected or confirmed.
- Authentication controls are bypassed or compromised.
- Audit logs indicate unusual exports, mass access, or credential abuse.
- Protected health information is disclosed to an unauthorized party.

## Immediate Response

1. Contain the incident by revoking affected credentials, rotating secrets, and restricting system access.
2. Preserve evidence by retaining application logs, audit logs, database snapshots, and deployment records.
3. Assess scope by identifying affected users, systems, data classes, and exposure window.
4. Document the incident timeline, responders, impact, and remediation steps.

## Notification Workflow

1. Notify internal security and compliance owners as soon as the incident is confirmed.
2. If protected health information was exposed, assess whether the event meets HIPAA/HITECH breach-notification thresholds.
3. Notify affected individuals, regulators, and media when required by law and scale of impact.
4. For business-associate scenarios, notify the covered entity without unreasonable delay and no later than 60 days from discovery.

## Recovery and Follow-Up

1. Validate that compromised access paths are closed and monitoring is restored.
2. Add or update automated tests, alerts, and access controls to prevent recurrence.
3. Record final root cause, corrective actions, and any required policy updates.
