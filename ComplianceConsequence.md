# Enforcement of non-compliance for ElgHealth

This document discusses the potential consequences of non-compliance for ElgHealth.

Specifically, this document focuses on the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPPA), the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act (HITECH) which extends HIPPA's enforcemen, and Information Blocking under the 21st Century Cures Act.

## HIPPA

The enforcment for HIPPA are split into the following sections: Privacy, Security, and Breach Notification. Information for the following sections is obtained from: https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/index.html.

### Privacy Enforcment

Under the privacy requirements for HIPPA, penalties are split into two categories: Civil Money Penalties and Criminal Penalties.

Civil Money Penalties are fines issued when a privacy violation requirement is not met. Based on the factors of date, the knowledge of the covered entity of the failure to comply, and whether the failure was due to willful neglet, the penalty amount per violation ranges from $127 to $1,919,173.

If the failure of compliance was due to willful neglet (the covered entity knows of the non-compliant issue), then a criminal penalty may be issued, with fines ranging between $50k and $250k and jail time between 1 and 10 years.

Information for this section is from: https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/privacy/laws-regulations/index.html

### Security and Breach Notification Enforcment Enforcment

Similar to the privacy requirement, noncompliance, factored by date, the knowledge of the covered entity of the failure to comply, and whether the failure was due to willful neglet, can range between $100 and $1,500,000 based on the number of violations.

Information for this section is from: Section 13410 of https://healthit.gov/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/hitech_act_excerpt_from_arra_with_index.pdf

## Information Blocking

In the context of Informaiton Blocking, violators found to purposfully block information can have civil money penalties up to $1,000,000 per violation.
