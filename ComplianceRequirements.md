# Requirements of compliance for ElgHealth

This document discusses the requirements of compliance for ElgHealth.

Specifically, this document focuses on two key compliance laws/rules: Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPPA) and Information Blocking.

## HIPPA

The requirements for HIPPA are split into the following sections: Privacy, Security, and Breach Notification. Information for the following sections is obtained from: https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/index.html.

### Privacy Requirements

The requirements of privacy under HIPPA addresses use and disclosure of health information. Organizations subject to this requirement are defined as covered entities.

- Who is Covered Entities:
    - Health Plans: this is individual and group plans that provide/pay for costs of medical services.
    - Health Care Providers: includes all care providers that transmits health information electronically.
    - Health Care Clearinghouses: defined as entities taht process information from non standard to standard or vice versa. These are usually business associates working with providers or plans.
- Information Protected:
    - Past, present, and future individual physical and mental health or condition.
    - Provision of health care (prescriptions).
    - Past, present, and future payments of care.
    - Any other information that can identifiy individuals (PII).

Generally, privacy requirements under HIPPA is to ensure that health information is only used and/or disclosed by covered entities for:

- Treatment
- Payment
- Healthcare Operations

Writtent authorization from related individuals must be obtained before information can be used for Psychotherapy Notes or Marketing.

Information for this section is from: https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/privacy/laws-regulations/index.html

### Security Requirements

Security requirements under HIPPA ensures to protect health information stored or transmitted electronically through physical and technical means. These security requirements focus on the integrity and confidentiality of health information.

Regulated entites must:
- Ensure confidentialtiy, integrity, and availability of electronic PHI
- Protect against reasonable threats to PHI
- Protect against reasonable impernissible uses/disclosures
- Ensure compliance of the workforce (employess, contractors, administrators, etc.) under the entity

Covered entites are the same as those defined in privacy requirements:
- Health Plans
- Healthcare Providers
- Healthcare Clearinghouses
in addition to business associates that handle the data of the covered entities.

Information for this section is from: https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/security/laws-regulations/index.html

### Breach Notification Requirements

The breach notification requirement under HIPPA is to ensure that any breach that occurs to a covered entity is made known to affected individuals. A breach is defined as unauthorized use/disclosure of information protected by the privacy requirements.

If a breach was discovered by the affected entity, the entity must nodify individuals affected, the secretary, and the media depending on the scale of the breach (500 or more affected). Business associates who are affected by a breach must notify covered entites no later that 60 days from discovery.

Information for this section is from: https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/breach-notification/index.html


## Information Blocking

Information Blocking is defined as a "practice that is likely to interfere with the access, exchange, or use of health information" (HealthIT.gov). This practice was defined under the 21st Century Cures Act in 2016, and applies to providers, Health IT developers, and health information exchanges and networks.

Information for this section is from: 
- https://healthit.gov/information-blocking/
- https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-45/subtitle-A/subchapter-D/part-171/subpart-A/section-171.103

## Assesment with Relation to ElgHealth

According to the research above, ElgHealth is defined to be a Health Care Clearinghouse under HIPPA's privacy requirements. All privacy, security, and breach notification requirements in relation to clearinghouses apply to ElgHealth.
